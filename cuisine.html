Mesfouf — Sweet Date Couscous

A southern Tunisian specialty!

The sweeter cousin of regular couscous — made with fragrant dates, raisins, and almonds. A beloved dessert couscous unique to the oasis towns of the south!

🕐 30 min 😊 Easy

📋 Ingredients:

Fine couscous • Deglet Nour dates (pitted) • golden raisins • blanched almonds • butter • powdered sugar • orange blossom water

👨‍🍳 Easy Steps for Kids:

1Steam the couscous until fluffy and soft
2Mix in butter and a spoonful of powdered sugar
3Add the dates, raisins, and almonds on top
4Sprinkle orange blossom water and serve warm!
💡 Did you know? Mesfouf is served at weddings and special occasions in the oasis towns of Tozeur and Kebili!

Asida — Desert Honey Pudding

Traditional Saharan dessert

A warm, thick semolina pudding topped with date syrup and butter — the ultimate comfort food of the Tunisian desert! Simple, sweet, and absolutely delicious.

🕐 20 min 😊 Very Easy

📋 Ingredients:

Fine semolina • water • salt • butter • date syrup (or honey) • optional: ground cinnamon

👨‍🍳 Easy Steps for Kids:

1Boil water with a pinch of salt
2Pour in the semolina slowly, stirring constantly until thick
3Spoon into bowls and make a little well in the center
4Fill the well with butter and pour warm date syrup over it!
💡 Did you know? Asida has been eaten in the Sahara for over 1,000 years — it's desert energy food!

Makroud — Date Diamond Pastry

Famous south Tunisian sweet

Golden diamond-shaped pastry filled with a sweet date paste and fried or baked until crispy — then soaked in honey! Sold in every souq in southern Tunisia.

🕐 50 min 🍯 Sweet Treat

📋 Ingredients:

Semolina • butter • water • salt (for dough) • date paste • cinnamon • oil for frying • honey for soaking

👨‍🍳 Easy Steps for Kids:

1Mix semolina, butter, and water into a firm dough
2Flatten the dough and place a line of date paste in the center
3Fold the dough around the dates and cut into diamond shapes
4Fry until golden, then dip in warm honey right away!
💡 Did you know? Makroud from Tozeur is so famous it was given a geographic indication label by the European Union!

Brik — Crispy Egg Pastry

Brik à l'œuf

A crispy pastry leaf filled with egg, tuna, and parsley — fried to golden perfection! Tunisia's most famous street food and Ramadan favourite, enjoyed all across the south!

🕐 15 min 😊 Very Easy

📋 Ingredients:

Malsouka pastry leaf • 1 egg • canned tuna • fresh parsley • optional: harissa • oil for frying

👨‍🍳 Easy Steps for Kids:

1Lay the pastry leaf flat on a clean board
2Place tuna and parsley in the center, then crack the egg on top
3Fold the pastry into a tight triangle so the egg stays inside
4Fry in hot oil until golden and crispy, serve immediately!
💡 Did you know? The word "Brik" comes from the Turkish word "börek" from when the Ottoman Empire ruled Tunisia!

Couscous with Merguez

Southern spicy style!

Tunisia's national dish gets a spicy southern twist! Fluffy couscous served with merguez sausages, vegetables, and the famous southern harissa paste — a UNESCO-listed heritage dish!

🕐 45 min 🍽️ Main dish

📋 Ingredients:

Couscous semolina • merguez sausages • zucchini • carrots • tomato paste • chickpeas • harissa paste • olive oil • spices

👨‍🍳 Easy Steps for Kids:

1Cut the vegetables (with adult help!) and brown the merguez
2Make a rich tomato broth with chickpeas and spices
3Steam the couscous above the broth for 20 minutes
4Arrange vegetables and sausages on top and serve steaming hot!
💡 Did you know? Couscous was inscribed on UNESCO's Intangible Cultural Heritage list in 2020!

Lablabi — Spiced Chickpea Soup

Beloved Tunisian breakfast

A warming bowl of chickpeas in a garlicky cumin broth, poured over crusty bread — the ultimate Tunisian breakfast! Sold in steaming bowls at markets all over southern Tunisia on cold mornings!

🕐 30 min 🍽️ Breakfast

📋 Ingredients:

Cooked chickpeas • stale baguette • cumin • garlic • lemon juice • olive oil • harissa • salt

👨‍🍳 Easy Steps for Kids:

1Break the stale bread into chunks in the bottom of a bowl
2Pour the hot chickpea broth over the bread pieces
3Add chickpeas, cumin, and crushed garlic on top
4Drizzle with olive oil, lemon juice, and serve right away!
💡 Did you know? Lablabi sellers in southern Tunisia start serving from 5am when it's still cold outside!
